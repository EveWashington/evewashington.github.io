I am a rising Junior at Columbia College studying data science, originally from metro Detroit Michigan.

On campus I can probably be found chasing writers and sources as managing editor of The Eye, a campus feature magazine, reaching out to residents as a residential advisor, or volunteering with the National Society of Black Engineers as outreach chair.

Professionally I have interned as a data reporter and graphics creator and a workforce analyst. As I continue to gather experiences and skills I can’t wait to be a part of bringing innovation and reliable information to the media industry. Here are some of the best things I've made


COVID-19 Student Job Loss Analysis

I analyzed a database with over 60,000 job postings from LionShare/Handshake, then working with a visuals team produced and reported on trends in job market and industry changes due to COVID-19 for an enterprise story. 



What about Metro Detroit’s dams? Spatial Mapping with R

After Midland’s dams failed in May 2020, as a Planet Detroit feature I analyzed data from the national dam registry to breakdown the state of Detroit’s dams. 



Quest, 1st Place Winner, DivHacks 2019

I built the back-end for a web-based, real-time feedback tool for classrooms with Node.js as part of a team of four people



‘100 Years of The Core’ with Python Natural Language Processing

An archival project studying Columbia Spectator News Articles that mention Columbia’s Core Curriculum, with Python, Pandas, Matplotlib, Textblob, and NLTK. Part of an investigation for The Eye



Civvi, 3rd Place Winner, HackMIT 2019

I was a critical part of a four-person team that developed the front-end of a social volunteering app using React.js and React Native. 



Health Policy and Population Health of the Michigan Parkinson Population

As Chairperson of this intern project at Blue Cross Blue Shield of Michigan, I led a team to help the Michigan Parkinson’s Foundation by creating a database of 900+ long-term care providers for Parkinson's patients. We presented our recommendations to hundreds of BCBSM employees and executives, check out our presentation. 



College Scorecard Data Analysis

An exploratory data analysis on data provided by the Government College Scorecard. Charted the popularity of different majors across the Ivy’s from 1998-2019, with Python with Pandas and Matplotlib in a Jupyter notebook.

These personal and observational essays aim to showcase the small parts of life as a student on Columbia's campus. I also spent one year in charge of editing these types of essays! Read about the Scholar Lion, Slader, waiting for the bus, and leaving campus because of COVID-19.
